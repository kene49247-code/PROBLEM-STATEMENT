About Financial Literacy

1

Financial literacy means understanding how money works — how to earn, save, spend, invest, and manage it wisely.

Many young people are not taught money skills in school, so this website explains everything in simple language.

Financial literacy means having the knowledge and skills to understand how money works — how to earn it, save it, spend it responsibly, invest it wisely, and manage it throughout life.

It is an essential life skill that helps people make smart financial decisions and avoid common money problems. However, many young people do not receive proper financial education in school. As a result, they often struggle with budgeting, saving, or planning for their future. To solve this gap, this website provides simple, easy-to-understand lessons on everyday money topics.

The content is explained in clear, youth-friendly language, using real-life examples, interactive tools, and practical tips. Whether it’s learning how to create a budget, understanding the basics of investing, or making smart spending choices, the website guides young users step-by-step.

The goal is to help them become confident with money, make better decisions, avoid financial mistakes, and build a strong foundation for a stable and successful future.

Learn the Basics

1. Budgeting

Budgeting means planning how you spend your money. Good budgeting helps you save more and avoid wasting money.

2. Saving Money

Always save a part of your income. Even small savings can become big over time.

3. Digital Payments

Learn to use UPI, ATM, online banking safely. Never share your OTP.

4. Investments

Investments like SIP and mutual funds help money grow faster over time.

Money Tips for Youth

Save before you spend.
Track your expenses weekly.
Avoid unnecessary loans.
Never share your OTP or PIN.
Start investing early.
